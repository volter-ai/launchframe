# Launchframe — Brand Context

## Product In One Breath

Launchframe creates auditable launch workspaces for OSS developer-tool launches. It is for maintainers who need package, repo, site, policy, HN/Reddit, evidence, and launch-day work to stay reconstructable after agent-assisted preparation.

## Positioning Spine

- Category: OSS launch-control workspace
- Audience: developer-tool maintainers and small teams
- Pain: launch work gets scattered and overclaimed
- Mechanism: scaffolded workspace plus validator and evidence gates
- Differentiator: launch artifacts are collocated and reviewable
- Proof: public npm install, dogfood workspace, live site, and release evidence
- Honest limitation: validation is structural and human-reviewed; it does not guarantee launch success

## Language

### Words To Use

- auditable
- reconstructable
- evidence
- rehearsal
- owner approval

### Words To Avoid

- automated launch success
- guaranteed traction
- bot posting
- compliance
- production-ready

## Claims We Can Make

| Claim | Evidence | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Creates launch workspaces | public npx scaffold | npm package |
| Validates required launch files and selected-surface evidence | `src/validate.js` | source |
| Does not automate posting, comments, or votes | source and README | repo |

## Visual Direction

Light editorial, precise, tool-like, and premium without looking like a generic SaaS landing page. Use real site assets under `site/assets`.

## Launch Channels

### Hacker News

- HN title direction: literal, OSS/devtool-oriented
- Founder first-comment angle: why launch artifacts need to be reconstructable
- Expected objections: process theater, generic checklist, overclaiming validation

### Reddit

- Target subreddits: r/opensource first
- Native angle by subreddit: OSS maintainer workflow
- Communities to avoid: any community that disallows promotional launches
